# KR Records Retention Policy & Schedule
**Version:** 1.5 | **Effective:** August 12, 2024 | **Scope:** Firm-wide
**Policy Owner:** Richard Salinas (COO), Ivan Garces (CRO)
**Records Administrator:** Adam Daube

## Purpose
Kaufman Rossin is committed to effective records management to meet business needs and legal requirements. This policy provides guidance for identifying, maintaining, and disposing of records in a timely and cost-effective manner.

## Key Rules
- Default retention period for any unclassified record: **maximum 2 years**
- Most client and administrative records: **8 years**
- Actual destruction should occur 6-8 months after the end of the fiscal year in which the record is to be destroyed
- Electronic records must be maintained in a designated centralized server (F: drive)
- The firm operates in a **paperless environment** -- all paper must be scanned and originals shredded (exceptions: signed engagement letters, signed representation letters, legal confirmations)

## Records Classification

### Client Records -- Audit (Assurance)
- Financial statement audit, compilation, agreed-upon procedures, reviews: **8 years after lock-down** (lock-down = 45 days after issuance)
- Quarterly financial reviews (10Q): **8 years after lock-down**
- Substantive communications with client/authorities: **8 years**
- Client documents not supporting attest engagements: **return to client or destroy after audit**
- Drafts: **destroy immediately**

### Client Records -- Tax
- Tax returns and workpaper files: **8 years**
- Permanent basis items (current clients): **permanent**
- Permanent basis items (former clients): **8 years**
- Tax advice, opinions, recommendations: **8 years**
- Client documents not supporting tax return: **return to client after filing**

### Client Records -- Forensic, Advisory, Valuation, Litigation
- Forensic/advisory reports + work papers: **8 years after issuance**
- Banking validation/risk assessments: **5 years after issuance**
- Appraisals and business valuations: **8 years after issuance**
- Expert reports: **8 years after final adjudication**
- Legal pleadings, court documents, depositions: **8 years after adjudication**

### Client Records -- Estate & Trust
- Fiduciary income tax returns (1041): **8 years**
- Estate planning documents (current clients): **permanent**
- Form 706s, 709s (current and former): **permanent**
- Substantive communications (current and former): **permanent**

### Client Records -- Escrow Services
- Life insurance policies, closing checklists, client documents, bank reconciliations, premium notices: **all permanent**

### Client Records -- Accounting & Family Office
- Financial statements, bookkeeping, payroll: **8 years**
- Client documents not supporting accounting: **return to client on closing**

### Administrative Records
- Corporate documents, bylaws, licenses, shareholder agreements, policies: **permanent**
- Accounting records (GL, checks, bank statements, payroll, invoices): **8 years**
- Personnel records (all categories): **8 years**
- Insurance records: **8 years after term**
- Contracts: **8 years**
- Marketing records: **permanent**

### IT Systems
- File server snapshots: **3 months**
- Backup tapes (weekly and monthly): **8 years**

## Legal Holds
When litigation, subpoena, or government investigation is reasonably foreseeable, the policy is suspended and all relevant documents must be preserved. The Records Administrator implements legal holds. A record may be subject to multiple legal holds simultaneously.

## Compliance
All employees, contractors, and applicable third parties must adhere. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination. The policy is reviewed annually (June/July).

## Key Compliance Gap (from Discovery)
Per Adam Daube (March 30, 2026): "The retention policy is aspirational. A lot of people aren't conformant to it, and there's no easy way to measure compliance across the firm."
